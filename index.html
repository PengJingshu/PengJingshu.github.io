About

Research profile

I am currently pursuing an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University. Before CMU, I earned a B.Eng. in Energy and Power Engineering and a B.S. in Mechanics from Tsinghua University.

My research goal is to build robotic systems that move robustly in complex environments and interact intelligently with the physical world. I am particularly drawn to problems where mechanical design, sensing, and control must be co-designed rather than treated in isolation.

Across projects at Tsinghua University, Peking University, the University of Notre Dame, and Carnegie Mellon, I have worked on robotic locomotion, biomechanical estimation, sensor fusion, and research-oriented prototyping.

Research

Selected highlights

Theme 1

Bio-inspired locomotion

Studied amphibious and underwater robotic locomotion with a focus on integrated propulsion, mechanism design, and experimentally grounded performance analysis.

Theme 2

Biomechanics and estimation

Worked on center-of-mass estimation during non-cyclic human motion, combining marker-based methods with ground reaction force information for improved robustness.

Theme 3

Embodied systems engineering

Enjoy building full-stack experimental systems where mechanical design, controls, sensors, and software are iterated together.
